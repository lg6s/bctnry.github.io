An engine for visual novels, pt 0.

What is visual novel?

A visual novel is an interactive game, featuring mostly static graphics, most often using anime-style art or occasionally live-action stills (and sometimes video footage). As the name might suggest, they resemble mixed-media novels. Sound novels and kinetic novels are types of visual novels, although visual novels have its own distinction between the others.
-- from Wikipedia

Why another engine?

No specific reasons. Just for the sake of it.

In what language will it use?

Racket, mostly because it's by far the only language that I feel comfortable to work with. There's a python one which is pretty popular so racket should also be able to do the trick, maybe the language itself is not designed for doing these things, though.

Will it use OpenGL?

Maybe not. I am aiming for being cross-platform so I won't make bindings of DirectX, but I'm not going to use OpenGL either because I just can't get hold of it; I will stick to the `racket/gui` package at least in the beginning.
